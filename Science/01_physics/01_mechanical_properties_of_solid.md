## 📊 Properties Table

| Property | Description | Experiment | Depends On | Unit |
|----------|-------------|------------|------------|------|
| **Elasticity** 🔄 | Solid ka ability to return to original shape after deforming force remove ho jaaye. Rubber band ki tarah stretch karne ke baad wapas original shape mein aa jaata hai. | Hooke's Law experiment - Spring ko different weights se stretch karte hain aur extension measure karte hain. | Material ki atomic structure, temperature, aur impurities pe depend karta hai. | Dimensionless (unit-less) |
| **Plasticity** 🏗️ | Solid ka permanent deformation without breaking. Jab force remove kar dete hain to original shape mein wapas nahi aata. Play-doh ki tarah permanent shape change. | Tensile test - Metal rod ko gradually stretch karte hain until permanent deformation hota hai. Load vs extension graph banate hain. | Crystal structure, grain size, temperature, aur strain rate pe depend karta hai mainly. | Dimensionless (unit-less) |
| **Strength** 💪 | Maximum stress jo material withstand kar sakta hai before breaking. Kitna force lagane pe material toot jaayega ye batata hai. | Universal Testing Machine se tensile, compressive, aur shear tests karte hain. Maximum load record karte hain. | Material composition, heat treatment, grain size, aur defects pe depend karta hai primarily. | Pa (Pascal) or N/m² |
| **Hardness** ⚡ | Resistance to surface deformation ya scratching. Diamond sabse hard material hai, easily scratch nahi hota hai. | Rockwell, Brinell, Vickers hardness tests - indenter se surface pe impression banate hain aur measure karte hain. | Crystal structure, bonding strength, aur microstructure pe depend karta hai basically. | HRC, HRB, HV (different scales) |
| **Toughness** 🛡️ | Energy absorption capacity before fracture. Brittle materials low toughness, ductile materials high toughness dikhate hain generally. | Charpy impact test - pendulum se specimen ko hit karte hain aur absorbed energy measure karte hain. | Material microstructure, temperature, aur loading rate pe depend karta hai significantly. | J/m³ (Joule per cubic meter) |
| **Brittleness** 💥 | Tendency to fracture without significant plastic deformation. Glass jaise materials brittle hote hain, suddenly break ho jaate hain. | Three-point bending test - specimen ko bend karte hain aur fracture behavior observe karte hain carefully. | Crystal structure, grain boundaries, aur stress concentrations pe depend karta hai mainly. | Dimensionless (unit-less) |
| **Ductility** 🌊 | Ability to undergo large plastic deformation under tensile stress. Copper wire drawing mein ductility important hai process ke liye. | Tensile test - % elongation aur % reduction in area measure karte hain fracture ke time pe. | Temperature, strain rate, aur crystal structure pe depend karta hai primarily for metals. | % (Percentage) |
| **Malleability** 🔨 | Ability to be hammered or rolled into thin sheets without breaking. Gold ki malleability bahut high hai, thin sheets ban jaati hai. | Hammering test ya rolling test - material ko flatten karte hain aur cracking check karte hain regularly. | Crystal structure, grain size, aur temperature pe depend karta hai mainly for processing. | Dimensionless (unit-less) |
| **Fatigue Resistance** 🔄 | Ability to withstand repeated loading and unloading cycles without failure. Bridges aur aircraft parts mein important property hai. | Rotating beam fatigue test - cyclic loading apply karte hain aur failure cycles count karte hain accurately. | Stress amplitude, surface finish, aur environmental conditions pe depend karta hai significantly. | Cycles to failure (N) |
| **Creep Resistance** 🐌 | Ability to resist slow deformation under constant stress at high temperature. Turbine blades mein creep resistance important hai. | Creep test - constant load apply karte hain elevated temperature pe aur time vs strain plot karte hain. | Temperature, stress level, aur material microstructure pe depend karta hai mainly for applications. | Time units (hours/years) |

## 📈 Key Relationships

**Stress-Strain Curve** 📊
- **Elastic Region**: Linear relationship between stress aur strain
- **Yield Point**: Plastic deformation start hota hai yahan pe
- **Ultimate Strength**: Maximum stress point on curve
- **Fracture Point**: Material completely break ho jaata hai

## 🔬 Material Examples

| Material | Strong Properties | Weak Properties |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Steel** 🏗️ | High strength, good toughness | Corrosion susceptible |
| **Glass** 🪟 | High hardness, chemically inert | Very brittle, low toughness |
| **Rubber** 🎈 | High elasticity, good flexibility | Low strength, temperature sensitive |
| **Aluminum** ✈️ | Light weight, corrosion resistant | Lower strength than steel |

## 💡 Practical Applications

```
    🏢 Civil Engineering     🚗 Automotive Industry     ✈️ Aerospace
    
    Bridge design mein       Car body materials        Aircraft components
    strength important       mein ductility aur        mein fatigue resistance
    hai load bearing         toughness important       aur creep resistance
    structures ke liye       hai safety ke liye       critical hai safety
```

## 🎯 Remember These Points

- **Elasticity** aur **Plasticity** opposite properties hain materials mein
- **Hardness** surface property hai, **Toughness** bulk property hai
- **Ductility** tension mein, **Malleability** compression mein important hai
- Temperature increase se generally **Strength** decrease hota hai
- **Fatigue** failure sudden hota hai without warning signs
